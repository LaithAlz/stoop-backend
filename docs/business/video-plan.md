# Marketing Video Plan — Higgsfield production

> **Status:** Planned 2026-06-12, ready to execute when the Higgsfield MCP
> is connected. Brand rules apply everywhere: plain English (no "triage"),
> no "founding/cohort/scale" tells, prices exactly as locked
> ($5/mo early access · locked for life), Heritage palette for end cards
> (canvas #FDFCFB, forest #2D4A3E, Fraunces for display text).

## Guardrails (non-negotiable)

1. **No synthetic humans presented as customers.** No AI-avatar
   testimonials, no fake "real landlord" quotes. People may appear as
   obvious dramatization (a sleeping figure, hands, silhouettes) — never
   as endorsers. Real testimonials come from real pilots, on camera or as
   text cards, later.
2. **No AI-generated product UI.** Every screen shown is a real capture
   (the app or `docs/mockups/`), composited over generated footage. Ads
   never show a product that doesn't exist.
3. **Claims match the ToS.** "Only a true emergency rings your phone" ✔ ·
   "never miss an emergency" ✘. "Drafts in your voice, you approve" ✔ ·
   "fully automatic" ✘.
4. Tasteful drama only — a dripping ceiling, not disaster porn.
5. Captions burned in on everything (most social video plays muted).

### Spot B — "When it can't wait" (the trust counterweight) · 20 s

Same opening grammar, opposite outcome — A and B together state the whole
severity contract, and B answers the instant objection "what if it's real?"

1. Same nightstand, 12:47 AM — message preview: "WATER IS COMING THROUGH
   THE CEILING LIGHT" (3 s)

   Emergency at 88 Dovercourt" (real call-screen capture) (4 s)

   "Stoop knows the difference." · [domain] (5 s)

## Cutdowns & secondary pieces

- **3 × 12–15 s vertical cutdowns** from A/B footage (no new gen):
  the silent-phone cut, the ringing-phone cut, and a "parking question →
  handled, you read a recap" lighthearted one. These are the FB/IG/Shorts
  ad units; A/B/C tested by verdict… by hook.
- **Product demo, 60–90 s, NOT Higgsfield:** real screen recording of the
  dashboard with the founder's real voice (or a clean VO) — lives on
  /early-access and answers "show me." Authenticity piece; zero
  generation.
- **Founder piece, 30 s, real camera, real Laith** — for the pilot
  outreach phase ("I onboard every landlord personally" lands 10× harder
  when a person says it). Phone camera + good window light beats AI here,
  full stop.
- **Checker promo, 10 s:** three quiz cards animating → "Is it an
  emergency? Find out in 30 seconds" → /is-it-an-emergency.

## Production notes for the MCP session

- Generate per-shot clips (3–6 s each), consistent prompt suffix for
  grade coherence: *"warm low-light interior, amber practicals, shallow
  depth of field, 35mm, cinematic, quiet domestic realism"*.
- Night scenes cool-dark with amber phone glow; morning scenes warm gold
  — the grade arc IS the story (night anxiety → morning calm).
- UI overlays composited after generation (real captures, Heritage or
  app-true styling); end cards built in brand, not generated.
- Voice: none needed for A/B (music + captions); if VO later, calm,
  unhurried, slightly dry — no ad-voice energy.
- Music: sparse, warm; the *silence* before B's ring is the best audio
  moment in the campaign — protect it.

## Measurement & cadence

- Every placement gets a UTM; Plausible + the waitlist `source` column
  close the loop (which video → which signup).
- Phase 1 (now → pilots): Spot A + demo on /early-access; cutdowns as
  organic posts. No paid spend.
- Phase 2 (post-pilot, with testimonials): real-landlord cuts replace
  dramatization in ads; consider small paid tests on FB ($5–10/day)
  against the proven hook.
- Iterate by hook performance, not by volume: 2 spots done well beat 10
  done fast.
