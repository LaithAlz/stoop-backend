※ How it works

Four steps between a 2 AM text and your eight hours.

No app for tenants to download, no portal to forget the password to. They text the same way they always have — Stoop sits in the middle.

STEP 01

Tenant texts

One phone number per property. SMS, photos, broken English, panic typos — all of it lands with Stoop first.

STEP 02

Stoop classifies

Emergency, urgent, or routine — judged against real criteria and Ontario's maintenance bylaws, with its reasoning shown.

STEP 03

Drafts in your voice

A reply written the way you'd write it — your tone, your tradespeople, your house rules. Held, never auto-sent.

STEP 04

You approve

One tap to send — with a 5-second undo. Every decision teaches Stoop and builds toward hands-off routine replies.

※ The severity contract

You wake up for floods. Not faucets.

The entire product is one promise: your phone only makes noise when the building genuinely needs you. Everything else waits politely for daylight.

Emergency

Your phone rings. Now.

Active water, gas smell, fire, no heat in a deep freeze. Stoop calls you, texts the tenant safety steps, and starts a timeline.

  • 40s · median time to your phone
  • 2/mo · how often this actually happens
Urgent

Drafted now, sent at dawn.

No heat overnight, fridge died, lockout. A reply is drafted immediately and waits at the top of your morning queue.

  • <30s · to classify and draft
  • 7:00 AM · waiting with your coffee
Routine

Handled. You read a recap.

Drips, parking, receipts, "is the gym open" — drafted from your house rules. Earn enough trust and these send themselves.

  • 80% · of all tenant messages
  • 0 · notifications they cost you
